5 Morphisms of Graphs

5.1 A Quick Start

A morphism of graphs (Also known as homomorphisms) is a function from the vertex set of one graph (say G) to the vertex set of another graph (say H), such that some properties (adjacency for instance) are preseved. In YAGS such a function is represented by a list. For instance, the list f=[2,3,4,3] represents a morphism that maps vertex 1 of G onto vertex 2 of H and also maps 2 to 3, 3 to 4 and 4 to 3. Also implicitly, f says that G has 4 exactly vertices and that H has at least 4 vertices.

YAGS has a very rich and flexible set of methods to deal with graph morphisms which we describe in the next sections. Here we describe only the most used ones.

Let us start by explaining three methods: FullMonoMorphism, FullMonoMorphisms and NextFullMonoMorphism. All three of these methods refer to the same kind of morphisms, which are Full- (the preimage of an edge contains at least one ede) Mono- (the images of different vertices are different) and Morphisms (the image of an edge is an edge). The differences among them are as follows:

FullMonoMorphism(G,H) Returns one morphism from G to H
FullMonoMorphisms(G,H) Returns all the morphisms from G to H
NextFullMonoMorphism(G,H,f) Returns one morphism from G to H:
the next one, after f
